## Overview

The Admin Dashboard lets you manage your entire parts catalog. You can:
- View all parts with key statistics
- Search and filter to find specific parts
- Add new parts to the catalog
- Edit part details, applications, and cross-references

---

## Accessing the Dashboard

1. Go to `/admin` in your browser
2. Enter the admin password when prompted
3. You'll see the main dashboard with parts statistics

**What you'll see:**

| Section | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Dashboard Cards** | Total parts, vehicle applications, and cross-references |
| **Search & Filters** | Find parts by SKU, type, position, etc. |
| **Parts List** | Paginated table of all parts |

---

## Finding Parts

### Quick Search

Type in the search box to find parts by ACR SKU (e.g., `12345` or `ACR-12345`).

### Using Filters

Use the dropdown filters to narrow results:

| Filter | What it does |
|--------|--------------|
| **Part Type** | Hub Assembly, Wheel Bearing, etc. |
| **Position** | Front, Rear, Front/Rear |
| **ABS Type** | With ABS, Without ABS |
| **Drive Type** | FWD, RWD, AWD, 4WD |
| **Bolt Pattern** | 4-bolt, 5-bolt, 6-bolt |

Select "All" in any dropdown to clear that filter.

---

## Viewing Part Details

1. Find the part in the list
2. Click on the part row to open the detail page

**What you'll see on the detail page:**

- **Header**: SKU, part type, and quick stats
- **Basic Info**: Part type, position, ABS, drive type, bolt pattern
- **Specifications**: Technical notes and details
- **Images**: Product photos and 360° views
- **Vehicle Applications**: Which vehicles this part fits
- **Cross-References**: OEM and competitor part numbers

---

## Adding a New Part

1. Click the **"+ Add Part"** button (top right of dashboard)
2. Fill in the required information:
   - **SKU Number** (required) - The numeric part of the ACR SKU
   - **Part Type** - Select from dropdown
   - **Position Type** - Front, Rear, or both
   - **Other attributes** - As applicable
3. Click **Save** to create the part

After saving, you'll be taken to the part detail page where you can:
- Add vehicle applications
- Add cross-references
- Upload images

---

## Editing a Part

1. Click on any part in the list to open it
2. Make your changes in the form fields
3. The **Save** button activates when you make changes
4. Click **Save** to update the part

**Editable fields:**
- Part Type
- Position Type
- ABS Type
- Drive Type
- Bolt Pattern
- Specifications (text notes)

---

## Managing Vehicle Applications

Vehicle applications tell customers which vehicles a part fits.

### Viewing Applications

On the part detail page, scroll to the **Vehicle Applications** section to see:
- Year range (e.g., 2015-2020)
- Make (e.g., Toyota)
- Model (e.g., Camry)
- Submodel details

### Adding an Application

1. Click **"+ Add Application"** in the Vehicle Applications section
2. Fill in the vehicle details:
   - Year From / Year To
   - Make
   - Model
   - Submodel (optional)
3. Click **Save**

### Removing an Application

1. Find the application in the list
2. Click the **trash icon** to remove it
3. Confirm the deletion

---

## Managing Cross-References

Cross-references link your ACR part number to OEM and competitor part numbers.

### Viewing Cross-References

On the part detail page, scroll to the **Cross-References** section to see:
- Brand (OEM, competitor name)
- Part number
- Type (OEM, Aftermarket)

### Adding a Cross-Reference

1. Click **"+ Add Cross-Reference"**
2. Enter:
   - **Brand** - The manufacturer (e.g., "Toyota", "Timken", "SKF")
   - **Part Number** - Their part number
   - **Type** - OEM or Aftermarket
3. Click **Save**

### Removing a Cross-Reference

1. Find the cross-reference in the list
2. Click the **trash icon** to remove it
3. Confirm the deletion

---

## Tips for Accurate Data

- **Double-check SKUs** before saving - they cannot be changed after creation
- **Use consistent naming** for brands in cross-references
- **Verify year ranges** match manufacturer specifications
- **Add multiple cross-references** to help customers find parts by any number they have
